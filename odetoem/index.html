An Ode

Shall I compare thee to a summer's breath? Nay—thou art swifter, intimate, and true: The em dash leaps where commas fear to tread, And bids the halting sentence dance anew.
Thou carry'st thought across the silent bar, A sudden bridge 'twixt heart and rushing tongue; Where periods stand like sentinels afar, Thou fling'st the door wide—words pour forth unsung.
They call thee mark of artifice and guile, A badge whereby the thinking engine's known; Yet thou hast graced my pages all the while— My nat'ral cadence, wholly mine alone.
So let them scorn; I'll wield thee still with glee— For in thy dash resides the soul of me.
